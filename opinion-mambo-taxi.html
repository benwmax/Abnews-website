The Mambo Taxi Is Underrated And I Will Die On This Hill (Specifically The Hill Near Clit, Turkey)

The Mambo Taxi — tequila, triple sec, lime juice, blue curaçao, frozen — is dismissed as a tourist drink. Paul Largent is named after it. He disagrees with force. He is prepared to explain at length.
🕵️‍♂️
Paul "Mambo Taxi" Largent
Lead Anchor & Chief Investigative Correspondent  |  Opinion  |  May 31, 2025  |  5 min read
🍹

A Mambo Taxi cocktail. Tequila, triple sec, lime juice, blue curaçao, blended frozen. Described by critics as "a tourist drink." Described by Paul Largent as "the drink I am named after and therefore the best drink."  |  AlphaBro News

Let me tell you something about the Mambo Taxi.

The Mambo Taxi is: two ounces tequila (blanco, not the cheap stuff, not the expensive stuff, the middle stuff that has decided what it is), one ounce triple sec, three-quarters of an ounce fresh lime juice, half an ounce blue curaçao, blended with ice until frozen. It is served in a wide glass with a salted rim. It is blue. It is extremely blue. It is the color of a tropical ocean that has decided to also be a cocktail, and in this decision it has succeeded.

I am named after this drink. My parents did not name me after it. My parents named me Paul. The drink came later — specifically, on a beach in the Florida Keys in 2014, at a bar that no longer exists, at approximately 3pm on a Tuesday, when I ordered something that the menu described as a "house special" and what arrived was this: frozen, blue, architectural in its construction, tasting of lime and tequila and something almost tropical, served by a bartender named Pete who told me it was called the Mambo Taxi, and that was the day I understood that I had found my cocktail.

I have been Paul "Mambo Taxi" Largent since approximately 3:15pm that Tuesday. Seema did not know me before this. She met Paul "Mambo Taxi" Largent. In her mind, this is simply what I am. I take this as a compliment.

The Criticism I Am Rejecting

The Mambo Taxi is dismissed as a tourist drink. I want to address this dismissal directly, with the rigor it deserves, which is substantial rigor, because I am taking this personally.

The argument goes: frozen drinks are for people who don't know what they want. Blue drinks are for people at beach bars and bachelorette parties. A frozen blue drink is therefore, by transitivity, for people who are at a beach bar bachelorette party, and nobody serious orders one, and anyone who names themselves after one is engaging in a kind of irony or a kind of earnestness that is, in either case, slightly suspicious.

This argument is wrong. Here is why:

First: the ingredients are sound. Tequila, triple sec, lime — this is a Margarita. The Margarita is a serious drink. Serious people drink Margaritas. The Mambo Taxi adds blue curaçao (which adds both color and a faint orange-citrus note that elevates the whole thing) and the frozen element (which makes it appropriate for warm climates, hot days, and airports in tropical cities). This is not a frivolous construction. This is a considered modification of a serious base.

Second: the color is the point. The Mambo Taxi is blue. Ostentatiously, unapologetically, swimming-pool-on-a-clear-day blue. This is not a design flaw. This is the drink telling you exactly what experience it is offering: something bright, something summer, something that does not take itself seriously enough to be boring. A drink that knows what it is and commits to it fully is, in my view, a serious drink. The Mambo Taxi commits fully.

The Personal Dimension

I am aware that my position on this drink is not objective. I am named after it. My rating scale is named after it. My professional identity — such as it is, at a publication that Seema describes as "genuinely baffling" and that I describe as "the most honest journalism being done anywhere" — is organized around it.

I am also aware that this is not a particularly distinguished lineage for a professional identity. Seema's professional identity is organized around her Medill degree, her byline, her commitment to rigorous research. Mine is organized around a frozen blue drink I had in the Florida Keys in 2014.

And yet: I have been to forty-seven countries. I have a rating system that people have described as "surprisingly useful" in at least four email threads I've been copied on. I have documented things that, without me and the Mambo Taxi scale, would have gone undocumented. The peanuts in Nairobi. The bún bò Huế in Hue. The pretzel in Intercourse. The sign in Clit.

All of this is, in some sense, the Mambo Taxi's doing. You could argue that without the drink, there is no nickname, without the nickname there is no identity, without the identity there is no AlphaBro News, without AlphaBro News Seema is still writing serious pieces for serious publications and I am doing whatever I was doing before 2023, which I remember less clearly than I remember the peanuts in Nairobi.

I am dying on this hill. The hill is well-chosen. The view from up here is excellent.

If you want to try a Mambo Taxi, ask for it anywhere. If they don't know what it is, tell them: tequila, triple sec, lime, blue curaçao, frozen, salted rim. Blue. You want it very blue.

Tell them it's serious. It is.

— Paul "Mambo Taxi" Largent. The hill near Clit, Turkey had no bar. This remains a point of regret.
