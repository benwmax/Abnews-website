Fucking, Austria Changed Its Name To Fugging And I Am Taking It Personally

In January 2021, the Austrian village of Fucking changed its name to Fugging. I had flights booked. I had t-shirts made. I had a whole bit. This is my formal grievance.
🕵️‍♂️
Paul "Mambo Taxi" Largent
Lead Anchor & Chief Investigative Correspondent  |  🇦🇹 Fugging (formerly Fucking), Austria  |  June 4, 2025  |  7 min read
😤
Paul Largent, somewhere in Austria, wearing one of the shirts. File photo. Photo: P. Largent / AlphaBro News
Paul Largent with a t-shirt that now reads "I've Been To Fugging, Austria" after an emergency edit with a marker. He describes the alteration as "an insult to the shirts."  |  AlphaBro News

I want to be clear that I understand, on an intellectual level, why the village of Fucking, Austria changed its name.

The signs kept getting stolen. Tourists kept taking photographs in front of them in positions that the residents found undignified. A global pandemic had, perhaps, clarified certain priorities. The village held a vote. They chose Fugging. Fine. Rational. Entirely their right as a community of autonomous Austrian human beings.

And yet.

I had flights booked.

What I Had Planned

Let me walk you through what I had planned, because I think it contextualizes my grief appropriately.

I had booked a direct flight from Chicago to Vienna, a train to Salzburg, a rental car from Salzburg, and a room at a guesthouse fourteen kilometers from the village. I had researched the best angle for photographing the sign. I had prepared three separate jokes — a short one, a medium one, and a long-form callback that required setup. I had, and I am not embarrassed to say this, practiced a facial expression in my bathroom mirror for the photograph.

And the shirts. I need to tell you about the shirts.

I had twelve t-shirts custom printed. White, navy lettering, very clean design: "I've Been To Fucking, Austria" on the front, the AlphaBro News eagle on the back. They cost me $340. They arrived on a Tuesday. On a Wednesday, the village announced the name change. The shirts arrived on a Tuesday. The announcement was Wednesday. I just want to be sure that's recorded clearly for the historical record.

An Open Letter To The Austrian Government

Dear Austrian Government,

I am writing to you from a position of genuine, if entirely self-inflicted, distress. You have allowed, or at least not prevented, the renaming of one of your most culturally significant villages. I appreciate that "culturally significant" is doing a lot of work in that sentence from the perspective of Austrian cultural policy. But from my perspective — that of a travel journalist who had flights, accommodation, a bit, and twelve shirts — the significance was enormous.

What am I supposed to do with these shirts?

I have tried several solutions. I wrote "formerly" above the word Fucking with a fabric marker, which Seema says looks worse. I attempted to sell them online, and four people bought them, which is simultaneously gratifying and depressing. The remaining eight shirts are in my closet. I wear one to bed sometimes. It is a complicated experience.

I recognize that you did not change the name of a village specifically to ruin my bit. I recognize that the residents of what was Fucking and is now Fugging have legitimate grievances about sign theft and unwanted tourism of exactly the kind I was planning to contribute to. I recognize all of this.

I am still taking it personally.

Regards,
Paul "Mambo Taxi" Largent
AlphaBro News

On The Name Fugging

I want to address the replacement name directly: Fugging.

Fugging is not funny. I want to say this on the record. Fugging sounds like the noise a wet boot makes on a linoleum floor. It sounds like a polite thing you say when you stub your toe in front of children. It sounds like a word that has given up. I have nothing against the village itself — I've now been there, and it is genuinely charming, alpine and green and full of people who just want to live their lives — but Fugging as a name is a demotion. It is a retreat. It is the name equivalent of changing your band's name from something dangerous to something that could appear on a children's cereal box.

The sign that says FUGGING, AUSTRIA is a beige sign with blue lettering. I photographed it eight times. Perfunctorily. There was no joy in it. There was certainly no bit.

What This Means For Journalism

I want to be clear: I went anyway. Because I am a professional, and because I had a non-refundable train ticket, and because Seema bet me twenty euros I wouldn't go after the name changed and I am not a man who walks away from twenty euros.

The village is lovely. The beer is good. The people largely ignored me, which is the correct response to someone photographing their town sign with the energy of a man who knows, intellectually, that something is funny but cannot quite access the feeling anymore.

I stayed one night. I drank two beers. I retrieved the twenty euros. I wore one of the shirts, and nobody said anything about it, which was either respectful or devastating — I'm still working out which.

Fugging: 4.3 on the Mambo Taxi scale. Points lost for the renaming. Points awarded for the beer, the mountains, and the fact that the guesthouse owner, when I explained my shirt situation, simply patted me on the shoulder and refilled my glass without comment. That, I thought, is hospitality.

— Paul "Mambo Taxi" Largent. The shirts are available on request. Four are gone. Eight remain. I'm not going to say how much.
