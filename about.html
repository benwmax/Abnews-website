Biography

Paul Largent was not supposed to be a journalist. He was supposed to be a marine biologist. Somewhere between his sophomore year and a spring break trip to Cancún that lasted three additional weeks, things changed.

Named after the cocktail — specifically the Mambo Taxi from Carlos O'Brien's in Cancún, which his mother was drinking when she went into labor — Paul has carried his namesake's spirit into everything he does, most notably his expense reports.

He joined AlphaBro News in its founding year after responding to a Craigslist ad that read: "Wanted: man willing to go anywhere, ask anything, embarrass nobody but himself." Paul was the only applicant. He was also the one who posted the ad, because he had already started the company.

Paul specializes in what he calls "geo-investigative humor journalism" — traveling to real places with funny names and documenting what he finds. His proudest professional achievement is confirming the existence of Clit, Turkey. His most viewed piece remains "Intercourse, PA: I Drove Four Hours For This." He has no regrets.

"People ask me why I do this. I tell them: someone has to. Then they ask who asked me to. And I change the subject." — Paul Largent, in his own words

Biography

Seema Krishnamurthy has a master's degree in journalism from Northwestern, six years of experience at a legitimate news organization she now refuses to name publicly, and the distinct misfortune of having met Paul Largent at a travel conference in 2021.

"He was pitching a story about Intercourse, Pennsylvania to anyone who would listen," she recalls. "I told him it wasn't news. He said, 'That's exactly what makes it news.' I have been trying to figure out if he's right ever since."

Seema brings genuine journalistic instincts to AlphaBro News, which mostly results in her asking good follow-up questions at places like the Dildo Interpretation Centre and writing 3,000-word essays about croissants that are, by any honest measure, extremely well-researched.

She covers food, culture, travel, and what she calls "the human side of absurdity." She is the reason AlphaBro News has fact-checkers. (There is one fact-checker. It is Seema. She fact-checks herself.)

"I didn't plan to be here. But I've eaten ramen in 32 bowls, filed from six continents, and once spent three days in a town called Dildo, Newfoundland. My career is going fine." — Seema Krishnamurthy
